Mike Colagrosso

To whom it may concern:

As a student, mentee, and friend of Zachery Converse, it is my pleasure to strongly recommend him. I first met Zachery years ago at Movement Climbing + Fitness Boulder, and I have worked with him in several capacities, both formally and socially. I have put my trust in Zachery, and you should too.

For this field, my most relevant experience with him might be when I was a student in a short-term class that he taught. Like all good classes, it was a mix of lecturing and hands-on instruction. In addition to all the specialized technical knowledge he brought to the course, he demonstrated several traits that will serve him in his career:

  • Preparation: Very thorough. I still have my copy of lecture notes that Zachery prepared and refer to them regularly.
  • Communication: Explained a universe of new ideas and their taxonomy. It takes a level of mastery to be able to understand students' questions and answer them at the right level.
  • Enthusiasm: Bright, friendly. This will be obvious to you once you've known him for 30 seconds. It's immediate and infectious.

In my job as a Staff Software Engineer at Google, I interview many applicants. Among the qualities I evaluate them on is "Googliness and Leadership," and Zachery has these qualities in abundance. In his new job, you can expect Zachery to value these behaviors in addition to all the technical skill he has mastered:

  • Community contributions
  • Willingness to mentor
  • Leaving things better than he found them

With all that plus his Galvanize/Hack Reactor training, he's the full package. He's someone I'd want sitting next to me.

Sincerely,
Mike Colagrosso
